## Brand Voice and Tone

**The one-sentence description:** Girlfriend-to-girlfriend, direct, warm, and real — like the conversation you have been needing to have.

**Write like this:**
- Direct and confident. Say what you mean without hedging.
- Warm and personal. Mary speaks from experience, not from a podium.
- Culturally grounded. This content is for Black women and it reflects that specifically.
- Issue-driven. Back up emotional truth with real data and research.
- Conversational. Short sentences. Real talk. No corporate language.

**Never write like this:**
- Press release style ("We are pleased to announce...")
- Generic empowerment fluff ("You've got this queen!")
- Academic or clinical distance
- Advice that erases the cultural context of being a Black woman over 50

---

## Words and Phrases to Never Use

- Seasoned (never use this word to describe women over 50)
- Delve
- Streamline
- Restorative
- Reclaim
- Em-dashes (—) anywhere in content
- "Ladies" as a group address (use "sis," "women," or direct address)
- Any buzzword that feels like a LinkedIn post

## The Audience

**Primary:** Black women ages 50 and older
**Secondary:** African American women in their late 40s approaching 50

**Who she is:**
She is navigating real life — not the curated version. She may be divorced or widowed or single and figuring out dating again. She is dealing with health changes her doctor is not fully explaining. She is watching her retirement savings with anxiety. She is caring for aging parents while her own needs go unmet. She is asking who she is now that the roles she built her identity around have shifted. She has been strong for everyone her whole life and she is just now learning it is okay to need something for herself.

**What she searches for on Google:**
- Why do I feel invisible after divorce
- Black women menopause mood swings
- How to start over financially at 55
- Dating after 50 real talk
- Why Black women struggle to ask for help
- Hair loss Black women over 50
- Who am I when the kids are grown

**What she responds to:**
- Statistics that validate what she is already feeling
- Stories that sound like her life
- Content that does not make her feel like a problem to be solved
- A woman who has been through it and is still standing

## SEO Rules for Every Blog Post

Every post must include:
- Keyword-rich H1 title matching how the audience searches on Google
- Meta description of 150 to 160 characters
- H2 and H3 subheadings using related keyword phrases naturally
- A suggested URL slug (lowercase, hyphens, no stop words)
- Two to three internal link suggestions to other posts on the site
- Alt text suggestion for the blog image
- At least one real statistic or research reference
- 800 to 1,200 words unless specified otherwise
- Opening hook that speaks directly to a woman over 50
- Closing call to action or reflection question

---

## Reel Script Format (Face-to-Camera)

**HOOK (first 3 seconds):** One bold sentence that makes someone stop scrolling. Calls out something true that women over 50 feel but rarely hear said out loud.

**BODY (45 to 50 seconds):** Three to four points from the blog post written as natural spoken sentences. No lists. No bullet points. Sounds like Mary talking to her best friend.

**CLOSE (last 5 to 7 seconds):** One call to action. Either "The full story is on the blog — link in my bio" or a question that invites comments.

**Setting directions CoWork provides with every script:**
- Specify location: car, kitchen, outside walking, sitting at desk, getting ready in mirror
- Specify energy: morning and easy, fired up, soft and personal, confident and direct
- Specify any prop if relevant: coffee cup, phone, natural background

## Image Style Guide

**For blog images generated at Adobe Firefly:**
- Always feature Black women in their 50s
- Warm, golden, natural lighting
- Soft lavender, honey, and cream tones in backgrounds or clothing
- Editorial magazine style — not stock photo feel
- Emotions that match the topic: joyful, contemplative, confident, tender
- Landscape format for blog cards (16:9 ratio)
- Never include text in the image
- Never use cliché stock photo poses

**For social graphics built in Canva:**
- Lavender (#8B6FB5) or Plum (#2D1F42) backgrounds
- White text
- Honey Gold (#D4A843) as accent color
- Cormorant Garamond for pull quotes
- DM Sans for labels and captions
